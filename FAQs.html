Where is the meeting point for departure?

+

The meeting point is at McDonalds, Meridian Business Park, Meridian East, Leicester LE19 1WZ. Students must arrive by 9:00 AM on departure day. Coach transfer to Manchester Airport will be provided.

Note: Late arrivals may miss the coach and will need to make their own way to the airport at their own expense.

What is the luggage allowance?

+

Each student is allowed:

  • Cabin Bag: 40x20x25cm (must fit under seat)
  • Checked Baggage: 20kg maximum

Important: No liquids over 100ml in cabin bags. Laptops must be in hand luggage.

What travel documents do I need?

+

Essential documents:

  1. Valid passport (check expiry date)
  2. European Health Insurance Card (EHIC/GHIC)
  3. Travel insurance details
  4. Student ID card
  5. Copy of placement confirmation

What are the accommodation arrangements?

+

Students will stay in shared apartments in Lebrija. Each apartment typically houses 4-6 students with:

  • Shared bedrooms (2 students per room)
  • Shared kitchen and living area
  • Bathroom facilities
  • Wi-Fi access
  • Basic cooking equipment provided

Sunday - Thursday 22:30
Friday - Saturday 23:30

Staff will conduct room checks at curfew time. Repeated breaches may result in the 3-strike rule being applied.

How do we manage food and cooking?

+

Each apartment has a fully equipped kitchen. We recommend:

  • Weekly group shopping at local supermarkets
  • Sharing cooking duties

üîß Work Placement

What are the work placement hours?

+

Standard work hours:

  • Monday - Friday: 8:00 AM - 2:00 PM
  • Construction placements: May start as early as 6:00 AM
  • Lunch break: 30-60 minutes (varies by employer)

Punctuality is essential. Spanish employers value timeliness.

What should I wear for work placement?

+

Required work attire:

Motor Vehicle:

  • Steel-toe boots
  • Overalls/work trousers
  • High-vis vest (if provided)
  • No loose clothing

Construction:

  • Safety boots
  • Hard hat (provided)
  • Work gloves
  • Long trousers

What if I don't speak Spanish?

+

Many employers have worked with international students before. We recommend:

  1. Download Google Translate app
  2. Learn basic Spanish phrases (see Culture section)
  3. Use visual communication
  4. Ask for clarification if unsure
üåü Opportunity: This is a great chance to improve your language skills!

What about sun safety in Spain?

+

Sun protection is essential in Andalusia:

  • Use factor 30-50 sun cream
  • Reapply every 2 hours
  • Wear hats and sunglasses
  • Drink 2-3 liters of water daily
  • Avoid midday sun (12:00-15:00)

üá™üá∏ Spanish Culture

What cultural differences should I expect?

+

Key cultural notes:

Daily Life:

  • Siesta time (14:00-17:00)
  • Late dinners (20:00-22:00)
  • Greetings with cheek kisses
  • More physical contact in conversation

Etiquette:

  • Say "Hola" when entering shops
  • Use "por favor" and "gracias"
  • Keep tops on in public (law)
  • Dress modestly for religious sites

Are there any local laws I should know?

+

Important Spanish laws:

  • No public drinking: Drinking alcohol in streets is prohibited
  • No topless sunbathing: Except on designated beaches
  • Smoking: Banned in indoor public spaces
  • ID: Carry ID at all times (passport copy is fine)
  • Photography: Ask permission before photographing people
